Growing up in the Lord

Sunday, Febuary 3rd, 2019

Serving what God has called us to do
In Hebrews 5:12, we are told that if we need someone to show us the path to God, then we
are no better than a child when it comes to knowing our Father.
From 1 Corinthians 13:11, to grow in God is to put away childish things, to behave as a
man, and to think as a grown up and to put away such childish behaviors and thinking.

What happens when we put up our childish toys? When we do, we don't always grow up.

We have our physical age, our mental age, and our Heavenly age. If we can't lead someone
to the faith, we need to grow our faith up more. We need to grow past the John 3:16s of
the Bible.

God intends for us to always grow. He will never have a stopping point in our life, no
point where we stop growing in our faith, in our knowledge, or in our life. He expects
and wants for us to always keep learning, to keep growing, to continue moving on the path
He set for us. In both our physical and spiritual lives.

Just by attending church on Sundays, or knowing John 3:16 from memory makes us a Christian.
God expects more from us then what we expects of ourself.

Spiritual growth requires good soil. Our lives must be prepared for spiritual growth in
order for growth to happen Mark 4:3-8. We have to change if we want to see God's light in
our lives.

Happy is the man who is always reverent, but he who hardens his heart will fall into calamity.
Proverbs 28:14

By tilling the soil of our hearts and accepting Christ into us, we let the
seed grow and flower, unlike the man who hardens his heart against Christ. Even if a seed is
furrowed, it will not grow in a harden heart's rocky soil where roots cannot survive.

A hard heart to the Lord will convince you that change and growth will never happen, just like a seed
that is sown on rocky soil. Even if seeds in Christ are sown well, without roots in the ground,
fear, doubt, or any sin will whither the shoots and kill the growth of our faith. If we don't plow the
soil of our hearts for the Lord, the Word will not take and grow.

Many honour me with their lips, but disvow me in their hearts Matthew 6:5

Part of growing up is also removing the competition in our hearts to give our everything to God. If
we don't remove or put to death the thorns and thickets of the worries and desires of our lives,
we will never grow in Christ. To grow, we must give our lives to Christ. If we allow what we want
to over shadow what God wants, we choke out the seeds of the Word.

Grow your faith in the soft soil of your heart but build your relationship with God on a rock hard foundation.

And we have known and believed the love that God has for us. God is love, and he who abides in love
abides in God, and God in him

1 John 4:16
